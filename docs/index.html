what the skill actually delivers

Taiwan Stock Radar is not just a screener. It is designed to help an AI agent move from broad market awareness to a ranked shortlist, then from a ranked shortlist to practical, risk-aware stock analysis.

scan the market

The skill reviews the broad Taiwan stock universe, checks market regime, sector rotation, factor strength, and risk filters, then narrows attention to a smaller set of high-priority names.

  • Useful for market-wide exploration.
  • Reduces random ticker-by-ticker judgment.

rank the top 20

The main ranking output is a top 20 candidate list. Each stock is scored through explainable components rather than an opaque headline number.

  • Surfaces names worth deeper work.
  • Supports explainable ranking instead of black-box picks.

judge one stock in depth

When a user asks about one ticker, the workflow expands into peer comparison, catalyst review, support and resistance context, scenario trees, and risk-aware planning.

  • Useful for shortlisted names.
  • Useful when the user asks about one specific stock.

define action zones

The output is designed to include direction bias, buy zone, stop or invalidation zone, and take-profit or trim zones so the recommendation can be reviewed and acted on more clearly.

  • Makes the output useful for watchlists and execution planning.
  • Keeps the recommendation tied to risk management.

why this skill is useful

A professional stock-analysis skill should not stop at “bullish” or “bearish.” It should help the agent produce outputs that are easier to challenge, compare, and improve over time.

clearer market coverage

The agent begins with the full Taiwan stock universe instead of improvising around whatever stock was mentioned first.

clearer recommendation quality

The top 20 list exists to prioritize attention. It helps the user understand why one stock deserves research before another.

clearer execution planning

The skill is built to return buy, stop, and take-profit framing so the result can support real research workflows instead of vague sentiment.

core workflow

The workflow is simple to describe and strict enough to reuse. This is what lets an AI agent behave more like a disciplined analyst and less like a generic chatbot.

1 load and normalize Taiwan stock data
2 compute regime, factor, and risk features
3 rank the universe and shortlist the top 20
4 define buy, stop, and take-profit zones
5 expand to a deep-dive memo for one stock

sample recommendation structure

A strong stock-analysis skill should produce a recommendation that can be reviewed, challenged, and improved. This is the kind of structure Taiwan Stock Radar is meant to support.

Stock: 2330 TSMC
Bias: constructive bullish
Reason: trend support, leadership quality, and stronger relative strength
Buy zone: near pullback support or controlled breakout confirmation
Stop / invalidation: below the structure that breaks the setup logic
Take-profit / trim: staged around prior expansion targets or overextension
Risk flags: market volatility spike, sector rotation failure, earnings-event gap risk
Confidence: medium-high, conditional on market regime staying supportive
